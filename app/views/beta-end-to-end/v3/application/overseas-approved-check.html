Gender recognition in approved countries and territories

If you have had your affirmed gender recognised in an approved country or territory, then you will need to provide official documentation that shows this.

This could be:

  • a document that’s equal to a Gender Recognition Certificate
  • an updated birth certificate
  • a court order
  • an entry in a legal register

List of approved countries and territories

If you have received gender recognition from somewhere else, or you can’t provide this documentation then you will need to provide medical reports and evidence of living in your gender for 2 years instead.

  • Australia (Territories of Australian Capital Territory and Northern Territory and the states of New South Wales, Queensland, South Australia, Tasmania, Victoria and Western Australia)
