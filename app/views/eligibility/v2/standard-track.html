You can apply for a Gender Recognition Certificate

You can apply for a Gender Recognition Certificate using the standard track.

Documents you need to provide

You will need to provide:

  • an original or certified copy of your full birth certificate or official confirmation of date of birth and birth gender (if outside UK)
  • a Gender Recognition Statutory Declaration
  • copies of all change of name documents if you plan to change your name
  • a medical report that includes details of your gender dysphoria diagnosis
  • a medical report that includes specific details of your treatment, or planned treatment

If you have ever been married or been in a civil partnership, you will also need to provide:

  • an original marriage certificate or a certified copy
  • spouse of civil partner’s statutory declaration, if you intend to remain married after receiving your Gender Recognition Certificate
  • a copy of your decree(s) dissolving the marriage or civil partnership, if you do not intend to remain married

If your spouse or civil partner has died you need to provide:

  • a copy of your spouse or civil partner’s death certificate

Examples of evidence you can provide

All documents that show you have been living in your acquired gender for at least 2 years must be dated and include your name.

  • driving licence
  • passport
  • a P60 or P45
  • benefits or tax letters and documents
  • bank statements and utility bills
  • letters from official, professional or business organisations like solicitors, accountants, dentists, doctors and employers
  • academic certificates or documentation
  • health care or identity cards including photo ID issued by an official organisation

What your medical reports must include

The first report must provide specific details of your diagnosis of gender dysphoria.

It must be provided by a registered medical practitioner or registered psychologist practising in the field of gender dysphoria.

The second report must include specific details of your treatment, or planned treatment, such as hormone treatments or surgery.

This report must be made by a registered medical practitioner but they do not need to practise in the field of gender dysphoria.

This means the report could be completed by your GP or surgeon.
