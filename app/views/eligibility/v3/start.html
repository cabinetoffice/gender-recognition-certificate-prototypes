Apply for a Gender Recognition Certificate if you want your affirmed gender to be legally recognised in the UK.

Although you do not need a Gender Recognition Certificate to get a new driving licence or passport with your affirmed gender, you do need one to:

  • receive a new birth certificate which lists your affirmed gender
  • have your gender updated in HMRC’s records
{{ govukInsetText({ html: 'Read more about how a Gender Recognition Certificate could impact any pensions and benefits.' }) }}

Applying for a Gender recognition certificate costs £5.

You can apply for help in paying this fee.

There may be other costs involved, such as legal or mediation fees that you may need to consider. Check if you can get Legal Aid to help with these costs.

This service is also available in Welsh (Cymraeg).

Before you start

You can use this service to find out if you are eligible to apply, and exactly which documents you will need to provide during the application.

Next steps

If you are eligible, you will be told which documents you need to provide. You will then be invited to start your application for a Gender Recognition Certificate.

As part of the application process you will be asked for both personal and contact details, as well as information on your circumstances, such as whether you are married or in a civil partnership.

You will then be asked to upload your documents, which will be reviewed by the Gender Recognition Panel. They may contact you if they require more information.

You will then be told if you will be issued with a Gender Recognition Certificate.

If your application is unsuccessful, you will be told how you can reapply or appeal the decision.

Decisions take around 12 weeks on average.
