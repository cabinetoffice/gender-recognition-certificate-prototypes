You cannot apply for a Gender Recognition Certificate using this service

You must be able to provide a medical report detailing your diagnosis of gender dysphoria to apply for a Gender Recognition Certificate online.

Alternative route to apply for a Gender Recognition Certificate

If you do not have a medical report that details your gender dysphoria diagnosis, you may still be able to apply for a Gender Recognition Certificate through the alternative route.

You can apply through this route if:

  • you’re 18 or over
  • you have a medical report that shows you have had surgery to change your sexual characteristics, or have gender dysphoria
  • you mostly live in England, Wales, Northern Ireland or Scotland
  • you intend to live in your acquired gender for the rest of your life
  • you entered a marriage or civil partnership in England or Wales before 10 December 2014, in Scotland before 16 December 2014, or in Northern Ireland before 13 January 2020
  • you’ve lived in your acquired gender for at least 6 years before those dates

You will need to post your application to us if you apply through this route.
