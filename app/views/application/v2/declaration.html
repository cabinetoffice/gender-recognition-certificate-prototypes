Before you start

You can apply for a Gender Recognition Certificate if you:

  • are 18 or over
  • have been diagnosed with gender dysphoria
  • are living in your affirmed gender and have been for at least 2 years
  • you intend to live in your affirmed gender for the rest of your life

If you were born in the UK, the Gender Recognition Panel will tell the relevant General Register Office about your application.

This will include telling them if you are married or in a civil partnership registered in England and Wales or Scotland and you are applying for gender recognition while remaining married or in a civil partnership.

If your application is successful, the General Register Office will contact you about your new birth certificate and, if appropriate, a new certificate for your marriage or civil partnership.
