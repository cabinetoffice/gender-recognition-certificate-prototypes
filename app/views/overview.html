Overview

Apply for a Gender Recognition Certificate if you want your affirmed gender to be legally recognised in the UK.

Although you do not need a Gender Recognition Certificate to get new legal documents like a driving licence or passport, this is not the same as legal recognition.

Having a Gender Recognition Certificate means you will have the same legal rights as anyone else in that gender.

It will also let you:

  • get a new birth certificate which shows your correct gender
  • retire and receive a state pension at the age appropriate to your gender

Read more about how a Gender Recognition Certificate could impact any pensions and benefits.

Gender Recognition Panel

After you apply, your application will be reviewed by a Gender Recognition Panel.

The panel is made up of people with either legal or medical qualifications. Their responsibility is to make sure applications for a Gender Recognition Certificate meet the requirements in the Gender Recognition Act 2004.

If your application is not successful, you will be told why and what you can do to resubmit your application.

Decisions take 12 weeks on average.

Fees

It costs £5 to apply for a Gender Recognition Certificate. If you cannot pay the application fee, you can apply for help.

Other costs you may need to consider during the application process:

  • statutory declaration witnessing, which is £5 for each declaration
  • ordering any official documents like birth, death and marriage certificates, which each cost £11
  • certifying any copies of documents
  • fees for medical specialists to write the medical reports you will need to provide
  • any legal advice or mediation you might need

If you need help with your application

There is detailed guidance on the application process and what is required. It also includes a list of voluntary organisations that can help you.

You can also:

  • contact Citizens Advice
  • find a legal adviser
